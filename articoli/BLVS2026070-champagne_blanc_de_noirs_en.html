Champagne Blanc de Noirs: white wine from black grapes, explained simply

A clear glass of wine can also come from grapes with dark skins: this is where Champagne Blanc de Noirs becomes interesting for those who really want to understand what they are drinking. The French expression means “white from blacks” and indicates a Champagne made from Pinot Noir, Meunier or both, vinified as a white wine. The juice of these grapes is clear; the dark colour is found mainly in the skin; therefore, contact between the must and the skins is limited during pressing. This explanation is useful because it avoids a common misunderstanding: a Blanc de Noirs is not automatically rosé, but a white Champagne originating from black grapes.

Champagne Blanc de Noirs: how to serve and pair Champagne from Pinot Noir

Champagne Blanc de Noirs: temperature and the right glass

Serving Champagne Blanc de Noirs well means respecting its aromas and structure. It is better to bring it to the table fresh, but not ice-cold: too low a temperature can close the aromas and make the wine less expressive. The ideal glass is not a flute, but a tasting glass or tulip-shaped glass, capable of giving the bouquet enough space. This choice helps especially with more complex Champagnes, where fruit, evolved notes and savoriness deserve attention.

Champagne from Pinot Noir: useful pairings and what to avoid

In pairing, Champagne from Pinot Noir likes flavorful but not overpowering dishes: roast chicken, veal, mushrooms, aged cheeses that are not too aggressive, rich fish and structured seafood cuisine. With Extra Brut, it is better to avoid very sugary desserts, because the contrast can make the wine feel too dry. Very spicy sauces or intense red meats can also cover the finesse of the bubbles. The best choice remains the one that enhances the body, freshness and finish of the wine.

FAQ about Champagne Blanc de Noirs and Champagne from Pinot Noir

What does Champagne Blanc de Noirs mean?

The most commonly used correct form is Champagne Blanc de Noirs. It means white Champagne produced from black grapes, namely Pinot Noir and/or Meunier. The wine remains clear because the grape juice is white and contact with the skins is limited during pressing.

What are the 3 types of Champagne?

It depends on the criterion. By colour and grape variety, a simple distinction is: Blanc de Blancs, Blanc de Noirs and Rosé. By dosage, instead, there are categories such as Brut Nature, Extra Brut, Brut and Demi-Sec. When buying online, it is always useful to read the product sheet.

What is the grape composition of Blanc de Noirs?

Champagne Blanc de Noirs is made from black grapes: Pinot Noir and/or Meunier. It can be 100% Pinot Noir, such as Devaux Cœur des Bar and Crucifix Père & Fils Épître Act, or 100% Meunier, such as Collard-Picard Racines Meunier Extra Brut.
