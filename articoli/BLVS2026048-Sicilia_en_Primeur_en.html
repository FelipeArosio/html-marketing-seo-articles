Sicilia en Primeur as an international preview

Sicilia en Primeur is the most important event conceived and organized by Assovini Sicilia. The event was created to bring the attention of the national and international press to the latest vintage of the island’s wines, alternating tastings, tours, masterclasses, conferences, and meetings with member producers.

Sicilia en Primeur 2026 and wine tourism: Sicilian wine speaks to the world

Why wine tourism is a concrete lever

The Nomisma Wine Monitor research for UniCredit, presented in Palermo, shows that wine tourism is now a significant economic asset. In Italy it generates around 3.1 billion euros for wine businesses, while in Sicily it takes on particular value because it combines wine, landscape, gastronomy, and hospitality.

The Etna case and the growth in tourist arrivals

Between 2019 and 2024, tourist arrivals in the Etna municipalities linked to the Etna DOC regulations grew by 17.4%, compared with 12.4% for the entire island. The figure confirms that the volcanic territory has become a strong destination for those seeking experiences connected to wine and local culture.

Who is the wine tourist interested in Sicily?

According to the companies involved in the survey, the wine tourist arriving in Sicily is often foreign, especially American, German, or English, with an average age between 40 and 55. They are not always experts: this makes clear language even more important, capable of turning a visit or online reading into wine culture.

Sicilia en Primeur and the market: why data matters for wine buyers

A complex international context

2025 was not simple for Italian wine: exports closed down 3.6% in value compared with the previous year. The contraction also affected other major exporting countries, a sign of international markets becoming more cautious in consumption and foreign purchases.

The positive signal from Sicilia PDO whites

Within this scenario, still PDO white wines from Sicily showed export growth in value of 2.4%. Toward the United States, the indicated increase is 8.4%, a figure that explains why Sicilian white wines deserve attention also from consumers buying online.

Production, denominations, and conscious choices

Over the last three years, regional wine production has settled at around 2.7 million hectoliters, with a PDO and PGI share close to 80%. Whites exceed 64% of Sicilian production: knowing these numbers helps understand why the island cannot be read through a single category, but through different denominations, areas, and styles.
