White pizza: creaminess, savoriness and a dry finish

A white pizza with cheeses, potatoes, vegetables or mushrooms has less tomato acidity and more room for creaminess and savoriness. Here, a drier sparkling wine may be useful, able to bring energy to the sip and contrast the softness of the ingredients without adding heaviness.

Wine and pizza with cured meats, sausage or speck: how to avoid mistakes

Cured meats and sausage: the wine should lighten the pairing

With cured meats, speck, sausage or pancetta, the risk is combining too much fat, salt and alcohol. In these cases, it is better to avoid very tannic or overly warm red wines. A Brut sparkling wine or a dry rosé can bring freshness and make the pizza easier to enjoy.

When to choose rosé or Extra Brut

Domino Rosé Vicara can work with more delicate cured meats and gourmet pizzas, thanks to its light red fruit and clean finish. If the topping is more savory or creamy, Vivaldi Blanc de Blancs Extra Brut offers a drier finish and greater structure. The rule is practical: the more intense the topping, the more the wine needs freshness, precision and persistence.

Wine and seafood or vegetable pizza: sparkling wine, freshness and savoriness

Seafood ingredients: a clean sip is needed

Anchovies, tuna, seafood or a pizza with shellfish call for a wine capable of respecting savoriness and marine aromas. Sparkling wines help because they do not weigh the palate down and leave it cleaner. Vivaldi Brut is suitable when you are looking for balance; Extra Brut is more appropriate if the pizza is more savory or refined.

Vegetables and mushrooms: choose according to the strength of the topping

With zucchini, mushrooms, artichokes or grilled vegetables, the decision depends on the base. On a light red pizza, a fresh Brut may be enough; on a white pizza with cheeses and mushrooms, Blanc de Blancs Extra Brut gives more depth. Domino Rosé remains interesting with vegetables, herbs and delicate ingredients, especially when you want a fruitier glass.

FAQ about wine and pizza

Which wine pairs with pizza?

It depends on the topping. With a Margherita or classic pizzas, a fresh and savory Metodo Classico Brut can work well. With gourmet pizzas or light cured meats, a Brut rosé is useful. With white pizza, cheeses or more savory toppings, a Blanc de Blancs Extra Brut can offer a drier and more precise finish.

Is wine or beer better with pizza?

There is no single answer. Beer is a common choice, but wine can work very well if it is chosen according to the dough, tomato, mozzarella and toppings. Sparkling wines are particularly suitable because they combine freshness, perlage and a clean finish.

What should you avoid drinking with pizza?

It is better to avoid overly alcoholic, very tannic or heavy wines with simple pizzas, because they can cover the tomato, mozzarella and dough. With spicy or very salty pizzas, also avoid wines that are too soft or sweet if they are not coherent with the topping.
