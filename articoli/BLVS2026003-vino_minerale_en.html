Mineral wine and tasting: how to recognize it in the glass

On the nose, a mineral wine often recalls flint, chalk, or a salty trail; on the palate, the most evident sensation is freshness that lengthens the sip. Common mistakes when perceiving minerality

It‚Äôs easy to mistake minerality for acidity alone: in reality, the savory component is different from a simple sharp sensation. Ideal food pairings with mineral wine

Dishes that enhance a mineral wine

With food, minerality works best when it meets clean flavors and precise cooking: fish, shellfish, and preparations with lemon or aromatic herbs. The reason is practical: freshness and a salty trail help reset the palate and keep pace with the dish without weighing it down.

When to avoid pairing with mineral wines

It‚Äôs better to be careful with very spicy dishes or those with sweet-and-hot sauces: salinity can amplify unpleasant contrasts. If the dish is particularly fatty or creamy, you may need a white with more volume or a wine with aging that provides greater roundness.
