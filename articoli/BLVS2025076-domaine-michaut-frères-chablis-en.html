A Family Story in the Heart of Chablis

In the heart of Beine, a small village nestled in the Chablis region, lies Domaine Michaut Frères. The story of this winery is deeply intertwined with the land and the meticulous work of a family that, for generations, has highlighted the mineral and precise character of its Chablis wines. The Michaut brothers grow only Chardonnay, the sole grape variety authorized in the Chablis appellation, crafting wines that faithfully reflect the identity of the terroir.

FAQ about Domaine Michaut Frères

Where is Domaine Michaut Frères located? ▶

The Domaine is located in Beine, a small town in the northern Burgundy region, part of the Chablis wine-producing area in France.

What grape variety is used in Domaine Michaut Frères wines? ▶

All wines produced by Domaine Michaut Frères are made from Chardonnay, the only grape allowed for the Chablis designation.

What is the difference between Chablis AOC and Chablis Premier Cru? ▶

Chablis AOC is fresh and mineral, ideal for everyday enjoyment, while Premier Cru comes from classified vineyards with greater structure and aromatic complexity.
