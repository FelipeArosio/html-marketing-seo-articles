Sparkling wine: complete guide to Traditional Method sparkling wines

What is meant by sparkling wine

Opening a bottle of sparkling wine means entering a precise world made up of pressure, fermentation, and production choices. The term sparkling wine refers to all wines that develop natural carbon dioxide during fermentation, creating a more or less persistent mousse in the glass. According to the information available on Vinoso.shop, sparkling wines represent a broad category that includes wines produced using different methods, united by the presence of natural effervescence.

Traditional Method and sparkling wine

When referring to Traditional Method sparkling wines, we are talking about a precise technique based on a second fermentation in the bottle. This method is also described in the sparkling wine selection on Vinoso.shop as one of the most recognized for producing quality sparkling wines.

Crémant d’Alsace Brut and French sparkling wines

Among Traditional Method sparkling wines, Crémant d’Alsace Brut holds a well-defined position. Produced in Alsace with secondary fermentation in the bottle, it represents a valid alternative to other well-known French sparkling wines.

Difference between sparkling wine and Prosecco

Many consumers wonder what the difference is between sparkling wine and Prosecco. The key point lies in the production method.

Conclusion on Traditional Method sparkling wine

Choosing a Traditional Method sparkling wine means carefully evaluating the production process and the result in the glass.
