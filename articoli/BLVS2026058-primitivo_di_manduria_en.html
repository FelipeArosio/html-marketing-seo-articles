Primitivo di Manduria and Primitivo di Manduria wine: what the denomination really means

Not all wines made from Primitivo grapes are automatically Primitivo di Manduria. The word Primitivo indicates the grape variety; Primitivo di Manduria, on the other hand, indicates a denomination with a specific production area and precise rules. This distinction helps the customer avoid buying only “a Puglian red” and instead choose a bottle linked to a recognizable territory.

Production area and rules to remember

The Primitivo di Manduria DOC production regulations cover municipalities in the provinces of Taranto and Brindisi. For the Riserva mention, an ageing period of 24 months is required, of which at least 9 in wood. In the case of Gloria Vigneti Reale, the Vinoso.shop product sheet indicates grapes grown using the alberello training system in the Manduria area and 12 months of ageing in French barriques, followed by at least 6 months in bottle.

What is the difference between Primitivo and Primitivo di Manduria?
