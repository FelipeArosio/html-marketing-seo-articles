Austrian wines and territories: Wachau, Styria and great European whites

Territory matters greatly in Austrian wines because it influences acidity, aromas and depth of the sip. The Wachau, along the Danube, is a useful area for understanding Austrian Riesling: slopes, terraces and rocky soils can produce taut, mineral and age-worthy wines. Styria, on the other hand, offers a different interpretation of white wine, with precise, savoury and aromatic Sauvignon Blancs.

Wachau and Styria in Austrian wines

Pichler-Krutzler Riesling In Der Wand 2022 comes from a terraced site near Dürnstein, accessible on foot via stone steps and marked by strong temperature variations between day and night. Tement Sauvignon Blanc Ehrenhausen Korallenkalk 2022 comes from vines between 15 and 35 years old on coralline limestone soils. Two areas, two grape varieties and two distinct ways of interpreting freshness.

Austrian wines and Sauvignon Blanc: Tement Ehrenhausen Korallenkalk

Anyone who associates Sauvignon Blanc only with immediate herbaceous aromas will find a deeper interpretation in Tement. Sauvignon Blanc Ehrenhausen Korallenkalk 2022 is a Gebietswein Südsteiermark DAC made from Sauvignon Blanc, produced in Styria with certified organic grapes. The Vinoso.shop product sheet reports 13% alcohol by volume, vintage 2022, Falstaff 94 and Jancis Robinson 16.5.

Korallenkalk: the role of limestone in Austrian wines

The grapes are harvested by hand and fermented spontaneously in stainless steel. The wine ages on fine lees in stainless steel and untoasted oak casks for 18 months, then is bottled unfiltered. In the glass, the most useful trait for the reader is the combination of limestone sapidity, clementine, yuzu, pink grapefruit and blackcurrant. It is a white wine suited to those seeking tension, aromatic intensity and a distinctive profile.

Austrian wines and Wachau Riesling: Pichler-Krutzler In Der Wand

Austrian Riesling from the Wachau should not be chosen by thinking only of the word “Riesling”. Pichler-Krutzler Wachau DAC Riesling In Der Wand 2022 is a white wine made from 100% Riesling, organic, with 12.5% alcohol by volume and 94 Falstaff points. The product sheet describes a steep terraced vineyard, worked entirely by hand, with soil rich in orthogneiss and a shallow topsoil layer.

Minerality and fruit in Wachau Riesling

In the glass, it shows a light chartreuse yellow colour with silver reflections, ripe mango, yellow peach and candied mandarin peel. The palate is taut, juicy, mineral and salty, with elegant acidity and a long finish. For a Vinoso.shop customer, these details help understand when to choose it: flavourful fish, raw seafood, non-sweet Asian cuisine, dishes with citrus or preparations that need freshness.

How to choose the right white wine for the menu

For a gastronomic aperitif, a fresh and mineral white wine prepares the palate without covering the food. For a more structured dinner, it is better to consider body, acidity and persistence. FAQ about Austrian wines

What wine should you drink in Austria?

In Austria, you can drink white wines such as Grüner Veltliner, Riesling and Sauvignon Blanc, as well as local red wines. For a Vinoso.shop path, the most useful focus is to start from Tement Sauvignon Blanc, Pichler-Krutzler Wachau Riesling and tasting boxes dedicated to Riesling.

What is a famous Austrian wine?

Grüner Veltliner is one of Austria’s most famous and representative wines. Wachau Riesling and Styrian Sauvignon Blanc are also important for those looking for precise Austrian whites, with freshness, minerality and profiles suited to cuisine.

What is the most widely planted grape variety in Austria?

The most widely planted grape variety in Austria is Grüner Veltliner. It is a central white variety for the country, often recognizable for freshness, citrus, a spicy note and great pairing versatility.
