Chianti Classico Riserva Docg: the role of Sangiovese in Chianti wines

In Chianti Classico, Sangiovese is the central grape variety. The Annata and Riserva types require at least 80% Sangiovese, while Gran Selezione requires at least 90%. For those buying on Vinoso.shop, this information is useful because it connects the wine’s profile to concrete elements: ruby color, freshness, tannin, structure and the ability to accompany flavorful dishes.

Types of Chianti wines and Chianti Classico Riserva Docg: Annata, Riserva and Gran Selezione

When talking about Chianti Classico, the three words to remember are Annata, Riserva and Gran Selezione. They are not synonyms: they indicate minimum aging times and different levels of selection. Annata must age for at least 12 months; Riserva for at least 24 months, including 3 in bottle; Gran Selezione for at least 30 months, including 3 in bottle.

Chianti Classico Riserva Docg and Gran Selezione in Chianti wines

Riserva is a suitable choice for those looking for a more structured red wine, yet still readable at the table. Gran Selezione, on the other hand, comes from estate-grown grapes and represents a more targeted selection. Alcohol content of Chianti wines and Chianti Classico Riserva Docg: how to read it

Alcohol content should not be interpreted on its own: balance with freshness, tannin and structure matters. Chianti Classico Riserva DOCG 2021 Cafaggio reports 14% vol and a recommended serving temperature of 18 °C. These are two practical indications to avoid common mistakes, such as serving the wine too warm or underestimating the weight of the dish.

Red wine, temperature and service in Chianti wines

At 18 °C, a red wine of this type expresses aromas and texture better, without weighing down the drinking experience. A large glass helps enhance the fruit and spice notes. For a dinner with red meat or aged cheese, the alcohol content of Chianti Classico Riserva Docg works together with the structure of the wine, not against the dish.

FAQ on Chianti wines and Chianti Classico Riserva Docg

What are the typical wines of Chianti?

The typical wines of Chianti are Tuscan reds based on Sangiovese. It is important to distinguish Chianti DOCG from Chianti Classico DOCG: the latter concerns the historic area between Florence and Siena and includes Annata, Riserva and Gran Selezione.

What are the best Chianti wines?

There is no single answer that applies to everyone. The best Chianti wines depend on occasion, personal taste, budget, vintage and pairing. For those looking for more structure and a profile suited to red meat or aged cheeses, a Chianti Classico Riserva Docg is often a very coherent choice.

How many types of Chianti are there?

Chianti DOCG includes Chianti, Chianti Superiore and subzones such as Colli Aretini, Colli Fiorentini, Colli Senesi, Colline Pisane, Montalbano, Montespertoli and Rufina. Chianti Classico DOCG, on the other hand, is mainly divided into Annata, Riserva and Gran Selezione.
