Vinitaly 2026: what it is and why it remains a reference point for wine buyers

A trade fair that helps you understand what to really look for

Unlike a simple festival with scattered tastings, Vinitaly was created as a professional platform. This aspect is also important for the B2C audience, because many trends that later reach shelves and online stores pass through here first: new regional projects, more targeted selections, vertical tastings, meetings with producers, and greater attention to specific consumption segments. In 2026, the event is also expanding some thematic areas dedicated to No-Lo Alcohol, spirits with Xcellent Spirits, and wine tourism with Vinitaly Tourism. In practice, observing Vinitaly 2026 helps you buy better: not by chasing vague fashions, but by recognizing bottles with a clear identity and a coherent profile.

Vinitaly 2026: dates, location, and useful information for planning

When Vinitaly 2026 will take place and where it will be held

The event is scheduled in Verona, at the Veronafiere exhibition center, from Sunday April 12 to Wednesday April 15, 2026. The calendar published on the official website indicates opening hours from 9:30 AM to 6:00 PM on Sunday, Monday, and Tuesday, while on Wednesday closing time is brought forward to 4:30 PM. For those who also want to experience the more urban side of the event, Vinitaly and the City is scheduled from April 10 to 12 in Verona’s historic center.

How to take part in Vinitaly 2026 without wasting time

Access to Vinitaly 2026 tickets is online only: the daily ticket costs 125 euros including VAT, while the four-day pass costs 280 euros including VAT, plus sales commissions. International operators who buy wine for work may request a free badge, subject to approval of their professional profile. The website also specifies that visitors without a direct purchasing role may visit the fair only on Sunday and Wednesday. Planning ahead therefore remains the most practical choice, especially if you want to alternate tastings, meetings, and the selection of wineries to monitor online.

Vinitaly 2026 and Vinoso.shop: three labels to keep an eye on

Antonella Corda Ziru 2021: amphora, tension, and substance

Among the bottles worth paying attention to is Ziru 2021 by Antonella Corda, an Isola dei Nuraghi Bianco IGT available on Vinoso.shop. A white wine made from native grape varieties, unfiltered and vinified in amphora, born in Serdiana. The aromatic profile reveals lime zest, pear, raw almond, and a clear mineral trace; on the palate it focuses on structure, texture, and lively acidity. The winery completed its organic conversion in July 2021. For this label there is also concrete confirmation in trade fair terms: an official document from the Sardinia Region on the allocation of spaces for Vinitaly 2026 includes Antonella Corda among the selected wineries.

Drei Donà Magnificat 2021: a Romagna Cabernet Sauvignon to follow

Another interesting name for Vinitaly 2026 is Magnificat 2021 by Drei Donà, a Forlì IGT Cabernet Sauvignon available on Vinoso.shop. This wine boasts recognitions from James Suckling, Robert Parker, and Wine Enthusiast, while the producer’s website defines it as a single-vineyard 100% Cabernet Sauvignon, coming from the first experimental vineyard of this variety planted in Romagna in 1984. It is a bottle suited to those looking for a red with structure, depth, and a precise style, more useful for an important dinner than for a distracted tasting. Drei Donà’s presence at Vinitaly 2026 is also indicated by trade sources and by articles dedicated to the Romagna wineries expected in Verona.

Cimarelli 2024: a very focused Verdicchio for those who want to drink well and spend the right amount

On the white wine side, Cimarelli 2024 offers a very convincing entry into the world of Verdicchio dei Castelli di Jesi Classico Superiore. The grapes are grown in Staffolo, between 380 and 400 meters above sea level, on clay-limestone soils, with soft pressing, controlled fermentation, and aging for eight months in stainless steel plus at least two months in bottle. The expected result is a taut and savory white, with notes of white peach, green apple, wildflowers, fresh almond, and a citrusy finish. For those following Vinitaly 2026 with attention to the balance between wine precision and accessible purchase, it is exactly the kind of label worth noting right away.
