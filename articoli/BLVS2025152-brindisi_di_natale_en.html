Christmas toasts in Italy: Christmas wine traditions, without stress

Christmas toasts and conviviality: what really matters

In Italy, a Christmas toast works best when it’s simple: a clear wish, an orderly gesture, and a well-served glass. There’s no need to perform or give long speeches. In practice: wait until everyone has a glass, raise it at chest height, and say a short line that fits the people and the occasion.

Christmas toasts around the world: habits change, not the idea

Europe: different phrases, the same gesture

In many European countries, the Christmas toast keeps the same structure: you look at each other, smile, make a wish, and then drink. The words change, not the meaning. It’s useful to remember this if you have international friends at the table: intention matters more than the perfect phrase.

Outside Europe: the toast as a group moment

Outside Europe as well, the toast is often a “transition” signal: people toast before starting, or at the peak of the evening. In mixed settings, the best solution is a neutral and inclusive formula: a wish for wellbeing, serenity, and a happy new year (when appropriate).

Christmas wine traditions and service: small details that change everything

  • Serve chilled, not ice-cold: so the aromas don’t get muted.
  • Pour without filling to the brim: leave room for aroma and bubbles.
  • When you can, pour for guests first and then for yourself: a toast is hospitality, not haste.

The Christmas toast moment: essential etiquette (without rigidity)

The right gestures: eye contact, glass, tone

Two rules make the toast feel more elegant without complication: look people in the eye and avoid creating confusion at the table. Many etiquette guides also suggest avoiding clinking cutlery against the glass to get attention: a simple “May I say something?” said with a smile works better.

When to toast: three moments that always work

  • At the beginning of lunch or dinner: to start and relax the atmosphere.
  • At midnight (if you celebrate Christmas Eve or New Year’s Eve): for a collective wish.
  • At the end of the meal: to close with gratitude.

Christmas wine traditions: how to make the Christmas toast special

Write (or improvise) the wish: short, personal, real

The simplest way to make a toast that everyone enjoys is to avoid generic lines and focus on something concrete: a quality of the evening, a thank-you, a realistic wish. It works even in two lines: “To this table, to the people who make us feel good, and to a peaceful Christmas.”

If there are multiple cultures at the table: choose universal words

When you have guests from different countries, avoid jokes that are hard to translate and prefer a clear wish: health, peace, friendship, serenity. That way the Christmas toast stays understandable and heartfelt for everyone.

Conclusion: the Christmas toast that people remember

The perfect toast isn’t the most theatrical one: it’s the one that makes everyone feel included. Choose a suitable wine, prepare a short wish, and raise your glass when the table is ready. FAQ about the Christmas Toast

What do you say when making a toast? ▶

A short, personal line always works. Examples: ‘To our table and the people we love’, ‘To health and a peaceful Christmas’, ‘To those who are here and those we carry in our hearts’.

How do you make a toast wish? ▶

Make the wish by looking at the people you’re addressing, with a clear and kind tone. You can mention the occasion (Christmas), the intention (serenity), and close with a “to your health.”

How do you make a toast? ▶

Wait until everyone has a glass. Raise it without sudden gestures, make a short wish, and then take a small sip. If the setting is formal, avoid clinking glasses; with family it’s more tolerated, but don’t overdo it.
