Gift a wine tasting: how to organize the tasting at home

A tasting box becomes more interesting when served with a minimum of order. There is no need to turn the evening into a technical course: clean glasses, the right temperature, small portions of food and a simple sheet on which to note colour, aroma, mouthfeel and favourite pairing are enough.

Serving order and small pairings

For a home tasting, it is best to start with the freshest and most linear sparkling wine, continue with the rosé and finish with the most intense or most distinctive bottle. With Champagne and Crémant rosé, salmon, tartlets, fresh cheeses, not-too-sweet red fruits and delicate crostini work well. With the Crémant d’Alsace box, instead, it is useful to compare Brut and Zero Dosage on the same dish, to understand how much dosage changes the perception of the sip.
