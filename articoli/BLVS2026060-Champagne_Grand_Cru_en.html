Why Grand Cru is not just an elegant word

In the case of Champagne, Grand Cru historically indicates villages classified at the highest level in the crus scale. The official Champagne.fr website explains that a cru corresponds to a wine-growing village and that the Champagne wine-growing area includes 319 crus. After the reform, the Grand Cru and Premier Cru mentions may still be used for the villages that historically benefited from them.

For the reader, this means reading origin as a practical piece of information: Avize, Oger, and Le Mesnil-sur-Oger are not label decorations, but concrete references to where the grapes come from.

Champagne Grand Cru and Blanc de Blancs: Chardonnay, Côte des Blancs and white wine without oak

When a Champagne Grand Cru is also Blanc de Blancs, the message is clear: it is made from white grapes, in this case Chardonnay. The Côte des Blancs is one of the areas most closely linked to this grape variety and appears in the Vinoso.shop product sheets for Franck Bonville, where Avize and Oger are central. This information helps anyone looking for a more taut, citrusy, and mineral profile.

Grand Cru Chardonnay: what to expect in the glass

Franck Bonville Brut Grand Cru Blanc de Blancs is produced with 100% Chardonnay from Avize and Oger. The product sheet indicates fermentation in temperature-controlled stainless steel and at least 30 months on the lees. In practice, the wine tends to focus on freshness, fine perlage, citrus notes, light brioche, and a saline accent.

Speaking of white wine without oak serves exactly this purpose: to explain a more direct style, where the tension of Chardonnay remains clearly readable.

Champagne Grand Cru and white wine without oak: what changes between stainless steel, oak and final style

Not using oak does not mean producing a simple Champagne. Stainless steel allows for a more neutral vinification, useful when the goal is to highlight citrus, chalk, freshness, and precision. Oak, on the other hand, can add breadth, vanilla notes, and a more enveloping palate, especially when used with balance.

Stainless steel and oak: concrete differences for buyers

The comparison between the three bottles in the brief is useful. Franck Bonville Brut Blanc de Blancs is made in stainless steel and maintains a fresher style. Pur Oger 2018, also by Franck Bonville, leads the reader toward a vintage Extra Brut, with a dosage of 4.2 g/l and five years on the lees. Dom Picard Collard-Picard, instead, is vinified in large oak casks with indigenous yeasts and without malolactic fermentation: in the glass it can feel broader, with notes of dried fruit and pastry.

When to choose the unoaked Brut Grand Cru

It is a bottle suited to those who want to start from the fundamentals of Grand Cru Blanc de Blancs before moving on to more vertical or more gastronomic styles.

Champagne Grand Cru Dom Picard Collard-Picard: oak, Extra Brut and a broader style

Dom Picard Collard-Picard shows the other path: the same 100% Chardonnay base, but vinification in large oak casks. The product sheet mentions Coeur de Cuvée grapes from Oger and Le Mesnil-sur-Oger, low-temperature settling, indigenous yeasts, and no malolactic fermentation. These are technical details, but they have a concrete effect in the glass.

What changes with oak

Compared with an unoaked Champagne, Dom Picard focuses on greater breadth, complex aromas, pastry, and dried fruit. The Extra Brut dosage maintains tension, while cellar ageing for at least four years adds depth. It is a bottle suited to those who want a less linear, more structured Champagne Grand Cru that is more suitable for a gastronomic table, without losing freshness.

FAQ about Champagne Grand Cru

Which are the Grand Cru villages in Champagne?

The Grand Cru villages in Champagne are historically 17. Among the most important for this article are Avize, Oger, and Le Mesnil-sur-Oger, linked to the Côte des Blancs and Chardonnay. Other Grand Cru villages include, among others, Cramant, Chouilly, Oiry, Aÿ, Bouzy, Ambonnay, Verzenay, and Verzy. For purchasing, the useful detail is understanding whether all the grapes in the bottle come from Grand Cru villages.

What is the difference between Premier Cru and Grand Cru?

In the historic Champagne system, Grand Cru indicated villages classified at 100% in the crus scale, while Premier Cru referred to villages classified between 90% and 99%. Champagne.fr recalls that the échelle des crus was abolished by the INAO, but the historic Grand Cru and Premier Cru mentions may continue to be used by the villages that benefited from them.

What does Grand Cru mean?

In Champagne, Grand Cru does not indicate a single vineyard as it may in other regions, but a wine-growing village historically classified at the highest level. For the consumer, it means paying closer attention to the origin of the grapes: Avize, Oger, or Le Mesnil-sur-Oger help understand the style of the Champagne before adding it to the cart.
