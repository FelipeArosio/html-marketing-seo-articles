Key characteristics of Ried Grassnitzberg Riff 1STK 2021

This Sauvignon Blanc comes from organically grown grapes on the hills of Südsteiermark, in Austria. On the nose and palate, it shows intense notes of red gooseberry, redcurrant, and Thai basil, supported by a pronounced herbal freshness. The body is medium to full, with a long, chalky, mineral finish that highlights depth and aging potential.

It is a vegan-friendly wine, bottled with a glass stopper, a technical choice that ensures aromatic integrity and consistent evolution over time.

Where does the excellence come from? Tement’s philosophy

Vineyards, terroir, and organic farming

Tement’s vineyards are located in Südsteiermark, a hilly area of southern Austria renowned for its limestone soils and constant ventilation. The estate works under a certified organic approach focused on sustainability, respect for the soil, and biodiversity protection. These conditions allow the production of healthy, concentrated grapes with a pure, clearly defined aromatic profile.

Winemaking method and the choice of a glass stopper

Winemaking includes spontaneous fermentations and aging on fine lees, a practice that helps build structure and complexity. The use of a glass stopper, beyond being a sustainable choice, ensures a consistent, airtight seal—ideal for preserving freshness, aromatic precision, and natural evolution over time.

FAQ about Tement Sauvignon Blanc Awards

What does “Ried Grassnitzberg Riff” mean? ▶

It is an Austrian geographical designation identifying one of Tement’s most renowned parcels, on chalky soils rich in marine fossils.

Why is the closure glass and not cork? ▶

A glass stopper provides a more precise, neutral seal, reducing the risk of taint and supporting optimal preservation over time.

Is it suitable for long-term aging? ▶

Yes. Thanks to its structure and natural acidity, this Sauvignon Blanc can age in bottle for several years.
