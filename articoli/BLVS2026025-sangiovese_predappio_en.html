Sangiovese Predappio: what Predappio really means within the Romagna DOC

Predappio as a geographical mention: useful rules for reading the label

First of all, Predappio is not just a town: in wine, it is an additional geographical mention (subzone) linked to the Romagna Sangiovese DOC. The Italian Official Gazette, in the appendix dedicated to “Predappio,” specifies that the mention is reserved for DOC “Romagna” Sangiovese wines (including Riserva) produced within the area defined by the production rules.

There is a technical detail that becomes useful when comparing bottles: for Romagna Sangiovese Predappio, the ampelographic base requires a minimum of 95% Sangiovese, with the possibility of other black grape varieties authorized in Emilia-Romagna up to 5%.

This is not theory: it means the character of the grape variety must remain central. If you are looking for a red wine where Sangiovese is clearly felt — fruit, acidity, tannic texture — Predappio is one of the most readable indications on the label.

Why Predappio is famous: from a name on the map to the glass

Predappio between history, tourism, and wine identity

Many people know Predappio for historical reasons: it is Benito Mussolini’s birthplace and, for that reason, it is often mentioned in tourism and cultural itineraries. In wine, however, its reputation is built on a different level: the Predappio subzone has become a reference point for those seeking a Romagna Sangiovese with personality.

In practical terms, this recognition translates into growing attention toward wines from the area. When the name Predappio appears next to Romagna Sangiovese, it indicates a clearly defined origin and gives you a clue about the profile: often more focused on freshness and precision than on easy softness.

Sangiovese Predappio: oak aging or amphora? The differences between styles

Madonna del Pruno in amphora: micro-vinification and wood “in the background”

If you want to understand how Sangiovese Predappio changes when wood is not the main player, the most useful bottle is Nota di Cantina Madonna del Pruno in amphora 2020. On Vinoso.shop it is offered as Romagna DOC Sangiovese Predappio and the “Sangiovese” page shows a price of €33.50.

A precious technical detail comes from the official Drei Donà shop: this “Nota di Cantina” comes from selected grapes from Vigna del Pruno, vinified and aged in terracotta amphora, with only one barrique used to complete the blend. The amphora portion is indicated as about 80%.

Translated for the drinker: amphora tends to keep the fruit more precise and to give a more direct sensation, while the wood component — in this case reduced — works more on balance than on aromatic imprint. If you are looking for a red with energy and precision, one that prepares the palate between bites without weighing it down, this is the most interesting path.

FAQ about Sangiovese Predappio

Why is Predappio famous? ▶

Predappio is mainly known for historical reasons (it is Benito Mussolini’s birthplace) and for the tourism connected to that theme. In wine, however, it is famous because it is an additional geographical mention (subzone) of the Romagna Sangiovese DOC, used to identify a precise origin.

What kind of wine is Sangiovese? ▶

Sangiovese is a black grape variety that produces red wines with clearly noticeable acidity and tannin, often very suitable for food pairing. In Predappio, the ampelographic base required by the regulations is a minimum of 95% Sangiovese, so the grape profile remains central.
