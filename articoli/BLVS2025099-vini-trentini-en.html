Trentino wines: alpine terroir and key grape varieties

Trentodoc: the mountain bubbles of Trentino wines

Among Italian sparkling wines, Trentodoc stands out for the Traditional Method, bottle refermentation, and lees aging that shape a fine texture. The grapes permitted by the regulations are Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, Pinot Blanc, and Meunier; elevation and a dry climate support acidity and clarity. On the palate you’ll find bakery notes, white-fleshed fruit, and a creamy mousse.

Trentino red wines: Teroldego, Marzemino, Lagrein

On the red side, Trentino wines offer clear identities. Teroldego Rotaliano shows dark fruit and defined tannins; Marzemino favors juiciness and fragrance, while Lagrein brings color and body. These expressions shine with home cooking and on the grill, offering versatility for everyday pairings.

The designations of Trentino wines

Trento DOC: the traditional-method heart of Trentino wines

Trento DOC protects the Traditional Method sparkling wines of the Province of Trento. The rulebook sets permitted grapes, aging times, and production parameters to ensure high standards. For online buyers, reading “dosage,” “disgorgement,” and “months on the lees” helps understand style and complexity.

Other Trentino DOCs to know

Beyond Trento DOC, the area includes designations such as Trentino DOC, Teroldego Rotaliano DOC, Valdadige DOC, and Casteller DOC. Each showcases specific grapes and zones: from Teroldego in the Campo Rotaliano to the red blend of Casteller, plus the mentions within Trentino DOC. Knowing the designation speeds up choosing among available labels.

FAQs about Trentino Wines

What wine is made in Trento? ▶

Trento produces Traditional Method sparkling wines under the Trento DOC (Trentodoc) designation. Permitted grapes are Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, Pinot Blanc, and Meunier; refermentation takes place in the bottle with aging on the lees.

What are the DOCs of Trentino? ▶

The main DOCs protected by the Consortium are: Trentino DOC, Trento DOC, Teroldego Rotaliano DOC, Valdadige DOC, and Casteller DOC.

What is a Trentino grape variety? ▶

Representative grapes include: Teroldego and Marzemino for reds; Chardonnay and Pinot Noir for Trentodoc sparkling wines. Lagrein and Pinot Blanc are also widespread.
