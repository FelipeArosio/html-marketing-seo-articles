Wine Enthusiast ratings and Brunello di Montalcino: why this denomination attracts attention

Why Brunello di Montalcino receives high scores

Not all Italian reds are read in the same way by an international guide. Brunello di Montalcino starts from a precise foundation: it is made from 100% Sangiovese in the municipal territory of Montalcino and follows strict production rules defined by the disciplinary regulations. The Consortium reminds us that Brunello is released for consumption starting from January 1 of the fifth year after the harvest, while the Riserva version starts from January 1 of the sixth year. This aging time, together with the tannic structure and the natural acidity of Sangiovese, explains why Brunello is often evaluated as a wine to follow over time. In Wine Enthusiast ratings, therefore, the score rewards not only immediate pleasure, but also depth, endurance, and aging capacity.

Brunello di Montalcino and Rosso di Montalcino: a useful difference for reading the scores

Those buying online sometimes confuse Brunello and Rosso di Montalcino because both come from the same territory and the same grape variety. The practical difference, however, changes how even a rating should be interpreted. Rosso di Montalcino has shorter aging times and a generally more direct profile; Brunello, on the other hand, is built to offer breadth, aromatic detail, and a greater aging perspective. For this reason, a high score on a Brunello Riserva carries different expectations compared to a more ready-to-drink wine. Connecting the numerical data to the denomination avoids superficial readings and helps the blog reader choose the right bottle for an important dinner, a gift, or a longer rest in the cellar.

FAQ about Wine Enthusiast ratings

What do Wine Enthusiast ratings mean? ▶

Wine Enthusiast ratings are scores out of 100 assigned by the publication during blind tastings. Each range corresponds to a precise qualitative judgment: 90-93 Excellent, 94-97 Superb, 98-100 Classic. They are meant to offer a quick and understandable comparison between different wines.

What does “90 Wine Enthusiast points” mean? ▶

It means that the wine falls into the Excellent category and is therefore highly recommended. It is not a marginal score: it indicates a high-level label, well made and worthy of attention, especially when considered together with denomination, vintage, and style.

Are 93 points on Wine Enthusiast a good score? ▶

Yes, 93 points are a very good score. They remain within the Excellent range and identify a very solid wine. The difference compared to a 98 does not erase the value of a 93: rather, it changes the ambition of the wine, the expected depth, and often the ability to sustain long aging.
